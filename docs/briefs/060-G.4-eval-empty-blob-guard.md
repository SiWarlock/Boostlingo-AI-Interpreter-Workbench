# /tdd brief — eval_empty_blob_guard (G.4; frontend defensive hardening)

> **Small, clean, deterministic frontend slice.** Hardens the WER-eval flow against an **empty/zero-byte recorded blob** — don't POST empty audio to the paid `/transcribe` STT endpoint; surface a clear actionable error instead. **Productive work while two FE slices are gated** (053-C2b on the 059 backend contract; the WER-fabricated-100% bug is diagnosis-gated pending a live repro).
>
> **⚠️ SCOPE HONESTY — this is NOT the WER-fabricated-100% fix.** The user's symptom is WER ≈ 0 (≈100% accuracy = hypothesis ≈ reference). An empty blob would yield hypothesis ≈ empty = **WER ≈ 1.0 (0% accuracy)** — the *opposite*. So this guard is almost certainly a SEPARATE issue; it improves robustness regardless of the WER root cause but does **not** claim to fix it. Do NOT bundle the two. The real WER fix lands later against a confirmed live fixture.

## Feature
After `audioCaptureController.recordBlob()` returns, reject a **null OR empty (zero-byte / below-minimum) blob** before any `/transcribe` POST — surface a sanitized, actionable error (`capture.failed` or a new `capture.empty`) and abort the eval cleanly.

## Things to flag at Step 2.5
1. **Empty threshold — `size === 0` only, or a small min-bytes/min-duration floor?** My vote: **`size === 0` only** (strictly deterministic; rejects the genuine empty-capture without risking a false-reject of legitimately-short audio). A min-bytes floor is judgment + could reject valid short phrases. Confirm; if you have evidence a near-empty (tiny header-only) blob also slips through, a small floor may be justified — flag it.

Confirm vs reusing `capture.failed` (zero new surface, but conflates "mic denied" with "recorded nothing"). Either is fine; your call on UX clarity vs minimalism.
